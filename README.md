# Amazon Prime Content Analysis using Power BI

This project presents an analysis of the Amazon Prime Video content library using **Power BI**. The goal is to explore content distribution, identify trends, and provide data-driven recommendations to support content strategy decisions.

## Project Overview
The analysis focuses on:
- Distribution of movies vs TV shows  
- Content availability across countries  
- Genre-wise breakdown with emphasis on kids and family content  
- Trend of content releases over the years  

An interactive Power BI dashboard is used to transform raw data into meaningful insights.

## This analysis answers the following business questions:
1. What are the different contents available in countries?  
2. List of titles directed by **Cannis Holder**?  
3. Which are the movie names released in **2021**?  
4. Use a **map** to show content distribution across countries.  
5. What is the **total count of TV shows in India**?  
6. List of **release years using slicers** for interactive filtering.  
7. Which **movies are listed for kids**?  
8. What is the **total number of movies and TV shows**?  
9. What is the **total count of ratings** for movies and TV shows? 

## Key Insights
- Movies dominate the catalog compared to TV shows.  
- Strong content presence in the **United States, India, and the United Kingdom**.  
- Wide variety of genres with significant focus on **kids and family-friendly content**.  
- Steady growth in content releases in recent years.  

## Final Recommendations
- **Increase investment in TV shows and original series** to balance the catalog.  
- **Focus on regional content** for high-performing markets like the U.S., India, and U.K.  
- **Expand kids and family-friendly content** to attract younger audiences.  
- **Prioritize high-performing genres** to maximize viewer engagement.  
- **Leverage interactive dashboards** for continuous, data-driven decision making.  

## Tools & Technologies
- Power BI  
- Microsoft Excel / CSV  
- Data Visualization & Analysis  

## How to Use
1. Download the dataset from the `Data/` folder.  
2. Open the Power BI file from the `Visualizations/` folder using Power BI Desktop.  
3. Explore the interactive dashboard to view insights and trends.
